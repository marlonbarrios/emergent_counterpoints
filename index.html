Emergent Counterpoints (Marlon Barrios Solano, 2026) v1.0

Inspired by the Counterpoint Tool from the Synchronous Objects project, based on the choreographic work of William Forsythe and the dance One Flat Thing, reproduced.
More Information

Shape — Click the Shape button and move the slider toward sameness so movers move in complete sameness. Move toward difference to see an irregular pattern: sometimes the same shape, sometimes symmetric or related, sometimes no match. Turn on the mark (⌒) to see when mover shapes match.

Speed — With shape on sameness, direct speed; move toward difference. With the ⌒ button on, the fan appears on a mover when its arms are in speed alignment (moving at the same rate); move the slider toward difference to see which movers have aligned arms.

More movers — Use + to add movers and build more complex motion.

Motion — Use Zoom to give movers more room, then direct motion. When movers move at the same rate and in similar directions, they leave trails. Turn trails on with the ⌒ next to Motion.

Flocking — Move the Flocking slider toward “strong” so movers are drawn toward each other and align their movement; some kinds of formations are created as they cluster and move together.

Collapse — Degree of collapse: at max collapse all movers superimpose at the same center (the only moment they can overlap); with different shape, speed, and thinness you still see them. At min collapse they repel and spread toward opposite directions.

Use the ⌒ buttons beside each slider to turn markings on or off. Layer different kinds of alignments to create complex counterpoint. Use reset to start another composition afresh.
